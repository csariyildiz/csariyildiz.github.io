Word Embeddings: These are mathematical representations of words that capture their semantic meaning, allowing NLP models to understand the relationships between words.

Named Entity Recognition (NER): NER is a task in NLP that involves identifying entities such as people, organizations, and locations from unstructured text.

Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging: POS tagging is the process of marking each word in a sentence with its corresponding part of speech, such as noun, verb, adjective, etc.

Sentiment Analysis: This is a task in NLP that involves determining the sentiment expressed in a piece of text, such as positive, negative, or neutral.

Topic Modeling: Topic modeling is a technique in NLP that aims to identify the topics covered in a large collection of text documents.


Preprocessing: Clean and preprocess the emails to remove irrelevant information and prepare the data for analysis. This may include removing stop words, punctuation, HTML tags, and converting the text to lowercase.

Named Entity Recognition (NER): Use NER to identify and extract entities such as people, organizations, and technological products and technologies mentioned in the emails.

Part-of-Speech (POS) Tagging: Perform POS tagging to identify the parts of speech of the words in the emails and extract relevant nouns and adjectives related to technology.

Topic Modeling: Use topic modeling techniques, such as Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA), to identify the topics discussed in the emails and identify those that are related to technology.

Keyword Extraction: Extract keywords and phrases related to technology from the emails and use them to create a technology profile of Enron.

Sentiment Analysis: Apply sentiment analysis techniques to determine the opinions and sentiments expressed in the emails about technology.

Visualization: Visualize the extracted information using techniques such as word clouds, network graphs, and heat maps to gain insights into the technological background of Enron.
