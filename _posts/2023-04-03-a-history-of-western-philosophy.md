As a philosophy enthusiast, I am a big fan of Bertrand Russell's ["History of Western Philosophy."](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_History_of_Western_Philosophy) Russell, a highly accomplished British mathematician, philosopher, logician, and public intellectual who lived from 1872 to 1970, penned several books on a diverse array of subjects.

Russell's comprehensive and engaging account of the development of Western philosophy is widely considered a classic in the field. His lucid writing style and ability to convey complex ideas in an accessible manner have endeared him to readers and scholars alike.

Russell's work has been praised for its rigorous analysis and deep insight into the philosophical ideas that have shaped Western thought. 

Through his exploration of the works of major philosophers such as Plato, Aristotle, and Kant, Russell provides readers with a comprehensive understanding of the evolution of philosophical thought over time.

## Some Criticisms Of The Book

* The book was written in Second World War. (1945) When it refers to "Modern Germany" it actually refers to Fuhrer's Germany.
* The book has been criticized for not mentioning Heidegger. It is known that Heidegger had good relations with the officials of the period.
* Too much concentration on pre-Cartesian philosphy. (Roger Scruton.)
* Lack of understanding of Immanuel Kant. (Roger Scruton)
* Not a academic textbook.
* A History of Western Philosophy was praised by physicists Albert Einstein and Erwin Schrödinger.
* Leibniz (Frederick Copleston)

## Introduction

Philosophy : Its conceptions product of two factors:
* Inherited religious and ethical conception.
* Scentific Investigation.

Philosophy occupies a middle ground between theology and science. Out conceptions can be divided into two categories: matters that we have definite knowledge and those wo do not.

* There is no definate knowledge.
 * Reason : That means philosophy.
 * Dogma, Authorithy : That means theology. (systematically developed religious beliefs and theory)
* There is a definate knowledge.
 * Science : It comes from scientific inquiry.
 * Dogma

* The philosophy is one of no man's land. It is not tied to any particular school of thought or doctrine with an approach to philosophical inquiry that is open-minded and willing to consider multiple perspectives.
* There are no fairytales. Commitment to empirical observation and critical thinking, rather than relying on fanciful or dogmatic explanations.
