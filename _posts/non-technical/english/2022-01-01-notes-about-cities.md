## Istanbul

* Largest of Turkiye and 15th of the world's. Its population is 15.46 million (Dec 31, 2020) which is more than many sitates. 
Istanbul is located in the northwest of Turkey, along the Marmara coast and the Bosphorus, surrounding the Golden Horn.

* The city served as an imperial capital for almost 1600 years: during the Roman/Byzantine (330–1204), Latin (1204–1261), late Byzantine (1261–1453), and Ottoman (1453–1922) empires.

*  A grave that was 8,500 years old was found at the Yenikapı excavations. [*](https://istanbultarihi.ist)

* When discussing the districts and neighborhoods of Istanbul, the Historical Peninsula, known as nefs-i Istanbul or Ders-aadet, which is surrounded by the Golden Horn, the Sea of Marmara and city walls, constitutes the epicenter. As for Galata, Eyüp and Üsküdar, known as Bilâd-ı Selâse, these were organized as juridical districts and integrated into the city over time; as such, they constitute a complementary aspect.

* With the Dersaadet İdare-i Belediye Nizamnamesi (Municipal Regulation), passed in 1868, Istanbul was divided into 14 municipal districts; these were Hagia Sophia, Aksaray, Fatih, Eyüp, Kasımpaşa, Beyoğlu, Beşiktaş, Mirgün, Büyükdere, Beykoz, Beylerbeyi, Üsküdar, Kadıköy and the Princes' Islands. [*](https://istanbultarihi.ist/451-districts-and-neighborhoods-of-istanbul-1453-1923)

* By 1930, Istanbul was comprised of 15 districts. These were Adalar, Bakırköy, Beşiktaş, Beykoz, Beyoğlu, Çatalca, Eminönü, Fatih, Kadıköy, Kartal, Sarıyer, Silivri, Şile, Üsküdar and Yalova.
